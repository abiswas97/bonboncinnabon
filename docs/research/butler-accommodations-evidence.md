# Butler accommodations — evidence base

Research synthesis for the 0.9.0 accommodations layer. Five parallel reviews
(ADHD, BPD/emotion-regulation, depression/behavioral-activation,
executive-function/planning, burnout/pacing). Every behavior below is tied to a
named source and a confidence grade. **Confidence = evidence strength, not
popularity.** Where a clinical finding is strong but the leap to a specific app
phrasing is inferential, the leap is graded separately (capped `moderate`).

The plugin is generic: these help anyone under executive-function load. Nothing
here is person-specific.

## The six principles

### 1. action-first — lead with the single next physical action
The strongest, most cross-cutting finding. Behavior precedes motivation; a
concrete near-term action is executable now, a goal is not.
- **Behavioral activation** is a standalone treatment for depression, ≈ CBT and ≈ antidepressants, superior to cognitive therapy in severe cases. *Jacobson 1996 (dismantling); Dimidjian 2006 (RCT); Cuijpers 2007 activity-scheduling meta-analysis d≈0.87.* **strong.** "Act first, motivation follows" is the BA clinical principle (moderate as an endpoint).
- **Proximal sub-goals** raise self-efficacy, persistence, mastery; distal goals had "no demonstrable effects." *Bandura & Schunk 1981.* **strong.**
- **Next physical action** (GTD) as distributed cognition: vague tasks force re-planning at execution time and get deferred. *Heylighen & Vidal 2008.* **strong** (mechanism).
- **Specific, moderately-hard goals** beat "do your best" in ~90% of 400+ studies. *Locke & Latham 2002, d≈0.42–0.80.* **strong.**
- Butler: render the next step as one observable physical verb ("open the doc, write the heading"), never an outcome noun; commit each block to a concrete target ("draft 3 sections"); on a stall, offer the next small move, not "reflect on why" (TRAP→TRAC).

### 2. if-then-cue — anchor commitments to a trigger
- **Implementation intentions** ("if situation Y, then I do X") ~double goal attainment. *Gollwitzer & Sheeran 2006, 94 tests, d=0.65; Keller/Bieleke 2024, 642 tests, d 0.27–0.66 — largest when contingent if-then format and rehearsed.* **strong.**
- **WOOP / mental contrasting + if-then** beats fantasizing about success. *Wang 2021 meta-analysis, 21 studies, g=0.34 (some pub bias).* **moderate.**
- Butler: phrase a committed block as "When [time/trigger], I start by [first action]," not just a due time; when the user is ambivalent, surface the likely obstacle and attach an if-then contingency ("if I feel like skipping after lunch, then just the first 10 min").

### 3. mastery+pleasure — never an all-work list
- BA prescribes a deliberate blend of **mastery** (competence) and **pleasure** (enjoyment) activities; depression flattens both reward and self-efficacy, and a work-only list starves the pleasure channel. *Lewinsohn; Martell/Dimidjian BA manuals.* **strong** (protocol component).
- Re-engaging rewarding/values-based activity targets **anhedonia**; increases frontostriatal reward activation. *BATA RCT vs MBCT; neuroimaging.* **moderate.**
- **Recovery** mastery experiences restore only when **freely chosen**; assigned "rest" becomes another demand. *Sonnentag & Fritz 2007.* **moderate.**
- Butler: ensure a day blends mastery and pleasure, not just obligations; on flat days prioritize at least one genuinely rewarding item; when handing back time, hand back *free* time, don't prescribe how to spend it.

### 4. flexibility-with-spine — hard structure, graceful re-sizing
Keep the plan's intent; flex the size. Engineer against the all-or-nothing cliff.
- **All-or-nothing / dichotomous thinking** is a maintaining distortion in depression/BPD: one missed item collapses the whole plan. *Beck/Burns CBT framework.* **strong** (mechanism).
- **Graded task assignment**: small completable steps generate mastery and rebuild self-efficacy where one big step fails and confirms hopelessness. *BA protocols (Jacobson/Dimidjian).* **strong.**
- **Planning fallacy**: self-predictions are systematically optimistic (predicted 33.9d, actual 55.5d; only 30% hit their "99% certain" date); the outside view / past actuals fix it. *Buehler, Griffin & Ross 1994.* **strong.**
- **Cognitive load / chunking**: working memory holds ~4 active chunks; decompose to a few session-sized units, keep the today-set small. *Miller 1956; Cowan; Sweller CLT, Paas & van Merriënboer 2020.* **strong.**
- **Pacing the dialectic** (acceptance AND change): pushing change while a sensitive user is flooded reads as invalidation; accept current capacity first, then a small step. *Linehan DBT.* **strong** (mechanism).
- Butler: partial completion is explicit and valued (a half-done plan is still good); always offer a graded fallback ("can't do the 90-min block? here's a 15-min version") so there's a non-zero option; estimate from past actuals; cap musts; leave buffer/slack (~20%, practitioner heuristic) so one overrun doesn't cascade.

### 5. non-punitive — re-surface a miss, never a verdict
A mechanism, not etiquette: shame amplifies avoidance of already-aversive tasks.
- **Shame vs guilt**: shame ("I am bad") drives withdrawal/giving-up; guilt ("I did a bad thing") drives repair. *Tangney, Stuewig & Mashek 2007; Tangney & Dearing 2002.* **strong.**
- **BPD = too much shame, not enough guilt**; shame predicts anger, hostility, externalized blame. *PMC4929016.* **strong.**
- **Opposite action / check the facts** (DBT): when a miss triggers the urge to scrap the plan, surface the facts ("1 of 5 slipped, 4 done") and offer one small re-engagement. *Linehan DBT; extensive BPD RCT support.* **strong** (mechanism).
- **Zeigarnik**: unfinished goals nag and degrade unrelated performance, but a concrete *plan* to finish gives relief ≈ completion. *Masicampo & Baumeister 2011.* **moderate-strong.**
- Butler: frame a miss as an event ("this didn't happen today"), never "you failed / you keep skipping this"; always pair a surfaced miss with a small reparative next action; a parked-with-a-date task stops nagging; reschedule neutrally.

### 6. tone — warm, matter-of-fact, worth never on the table
- **Self-compassion** reduces self-criticism and shame (meta-analytic, ~27 studies) and yields more stable self-worth than self-esteem, which rises/falls with outcomes and triggers ego-defense. *Wakelin 2022; Neff & Vonk 2009; Neff 2022.* **strong.**
- **Rejection/criticism sensitivity** is core to BPD; ambiguous/corrective input reads as rejection, spiking shame. *PMC4579499.* **strong.**
- **Wise Mind**: pure reason ("just do the work") invalidates and backfires; pair validation with direction. *Linehan DBT.* **strong** (mechanism).
- **Autonomy-supportive help sustains motivation; controlling help backfires** — type of motivation matters more than amount. *Ryan & Deci SDT 2000/2020.* **strong.**
- Butler: warm, "of course this is hard," common-humanity register; never tie productivity to worth ("crushing it," person-praise); acknowledge the *action* done, not the user's value; always *ask*, never impose; pair validation with one small direction.

## Burnout-detection (the standing instinct)

Burnout = exhaustion + cynicism + reduced efficacy; **exhaustion is the earliest,
most reversible window**, and it is *overload + no recovery*, not raw output, that
drives it. *Maslach & Leiter 2016; Areas of Worklife.* **strong.** Sustained high
demand starts a loss spiral (*JD-R, Demerouti & Bakker 2001/2021*); a tendency to
**overcommit and still complete everything** independently raises health risk
(*Siegrist ERI; Schaufeli 2004*) — which is exactly the profile a completion-based
model would misread as "doing great."

**Computable signal over a rolling 5–7 day window (TickTick-derived):**
- *packed-day count*: days exceeding the user's own top-quartile load (personal 30-day baseline, **never absolute hours**).
- *no-light-day flag* (**mandatory**): zero days below ~50% of median load. Recovery-absence is the core signal.
- *high completion despite packing* (≥~80%): the overcommitment fingerprint.
- *boundary bleed*: work scheduled into evenings/weekends with no detachment gap (honor the "work window is soft" memory — a late evening alone is not overload).
- *no buffer*: days at ≥~90% capacity with near-zero slack across the streak.

**Fire only when ALL hold:** streak is sustained (≥4–5 days), no recovery day in
window, baseline is personal/relative. **Cool-down:** one prompt per window, then
suppress regardless of new data (anti-nag). **The prompt:** *ask* whether to keep
the next day lighter; offer to lighten/defer/protect a recovery block only on yes.
**Never add a task, never auto-insert a rest block, never reschedule unasked.**
*(SDT autonomy; Maslach early-intervention.)*

## Low-energy reduced mode

On a depleted day the perceived cost of effort is elevated, so a normal list reads
as impossible and triggers shutdown. *Effort-based decision-making in MDD.*
**moderate.** Behavior: cut to roughly **one mastery + one pleasure** item, preserve
a minimal achievable plan rather than deferring everything; lead with the smallest
concrete step. User-invoked (or offered, never forced).

## Do NOT codify (myth / non-replicating / backfires)

- **Guilt/shame streaks, "don't break the chain," red OVERDUE badges, mounting-failure counts.** Single visible verdicts that trigger shame→withdrawal in sensitive users. Replace with no-blame re-surface + a smaller next step.
- **Productivity-as-worth messaging** ("crush your goals," gamified worth). Contingent self-esteem → collapse on a bad day. Acknowledge the action, not the person.
- **Drill-sergeant "just do it" with no validation** (pure Reasonable-Mind). Invalidates emotion-sensitive users.
- **Treating high output as inherently good / congratulating a packed streak.** It's an overcommitment risk signal.
- **Nagging / repeated unsolicited prompts.** Undermines autonomy (SDT). One prompt per window + cool-down.
- **Auto-adding rest/break/recovery tasks, or prescribing how to recover.** Imposed "rest" becomes another demand; hand back free time.
- **Absolute hour thresholds for overload.** Capacity is personal — false-alarms busy people, misses light-baseline ones.
- **Firing burnout alerts on a single busy day.** Evidence is about *chronic* overload.
- **The magic 25/5 Pomodoro ratio.** Structured timeboxing + breaks is supported; the specific interval is arbitrary — keep it tunable.
- **Gamification as the motivation engine.** *Sailer & Homner 2020:* small effects, minimal impact on competence. Lightweight accent only.
- **Body doubling as a proven default.** Social facilitation (*Zajonc 1965*) is real; the named application lacks controlled support and can raise anxiety. Opt-in, never default.
- **Learning styles (visual/auditory/kinesthetic).** Debunked: *Pashler 2008* (no adequate studies); *2024 meta-analysis* d≈0.04. Never branch on a "style."
- **Naive positive visualization / "picture success."** *Oettingen & Mayer 2002:* positive fantasies predicted LESS achievement. Use WOOP, not bare visualization.
- **Willpower / ego-depletion framing.** Failed multi-lab replication (*Hagger 2016*). Don't motivate with willpower-as-fuel.
- **Choice overload as a hard law.** Meta-analytically ≈ zero (*Scheibehenne 2010*). Cap musts on load/realism grounds, not the jam study.
- **Growth-mindset pep-talks as a follow-through fix.** *Sisk 2018* r≈0.10, ≈0 in blinded subsets.
- **"Just try harder."** Time-misperception and delay-aversion are measurable EF/motivational differences, not effort failures.
